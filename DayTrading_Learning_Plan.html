📈 Day Trading Styles (Pick One to Start)

Scalping

Very fast trades (seconds to minutes). Small profits, high frequency. Requires focus and fast execution. Not beginner-friendly.

Momentum Trading ⭐

Ride stocks making big moves on high volume. Hold minutes to hours. Best for beginners. Clear entries and exits.

Breakout Trading

Buy when price breaks above resistance with volume. Clear stop-loss levels. Good risk/reward. Beginner-friendly.

Reversal Trading

Catch stocks bouncing off support or reversing trend. Higher risk, requires experience. Not recommended for beginners.

📐 Chart Patterns (The Playbook)

Bull Flag 🏁

Sharp move up (pole), then slight pullback on low volume (flag). When it breaks above the flag = continuation. High probability pattern. Look for volume on breakout.

Double Bottom (W)

Price hits support twice, forms a "W" shape. Break above the middle peak = buy signal. Shows buyers are strong at that level.

Cup & Handle ☕

Rounded bottom (cup) followed by small pullback (handle). Breakout above handle = strong buy. Works on multiple timeframes.

Head & Shoulders

Three peaks — middle is highest. Break below "neckline" = bearish. Inverse H&S (three bottoms) = bullish. Classic reversal pattern.

Ascending Triangle 📐

Flat resistance + rising support (higher lows). Compression. Usually breaks UP. Volume should increase on breakout. Very reliable.

Opening Range Breakout

Mark high/low of first 15-30 min. Trade the breakout direction with volume. Simple, effective for day traders. Great for beginners.

⚡ Robinhood Pros & Cons for Day Trading

✅ Pros

Commission-free trades. Clean mobile UI. Fractional shares. Easy to start. No minimum to open account. Extended hours available.

❌ Cons

Limited charting (use TradingView instead). No Level 2 data without Gold. Slower execution vs. other brokers. No advanced order types. Limited scanners.

💡 Robinhood Gold ($5/month) — Worth It?

For day trading beginners: YES, eventually. Gold gives you:

  • Level 2 Market Data (Nasdaq): See the order book — who's buying/selling at what prices. Useful for reading momentum.
  • Professional Research: Morningstar reports on stocks.
  • Bigger Instant Deposits: Access to more buying power faster.
  • Margin: Borrowing money to trade — DO NOT USE THIS AS A BEGINNER. Margin amplifies losses.

Recommendation: Start without Gold. Get it after Week 4 when you can actually use Level 2 data meaningfully.

🔄 PDT Rule Workarounds (Legal)

  • Swing Trade: Hold overnight (not a day trade). Buy today, sell tomorrow.
  • Use 3 Wisely: Only use your 3 day trades on A+ setups. Make them count.
  • Multiple Accounts: You could open accounts at multiple brokers (Webull, Fidelity) — each gets 3 day trades. Legal but complex.
  • Cash Account: Switch to cash account (no margin). No PDT rule, but must wait for trades to settle (T+1). Limits buying power.
  • Get to $25K: The real solution. Save aggressively. Once over $25K = unlimited day trades.
Wealth Builder Long-Term Holds — Buy, Hold & Grow

💡 Why Long-Term Investing Matters

Day trading is income. Long-term investing is wealth. While you're learning to day trade, you should ALSO be building a portfolio of quality stocks/ETFs that compound over years. The S&P 500 averages ~10% annually. $500/month at 10% = $1,000,000+ in 25 years. Let time do the heavy lifting.

🎯 Criteria for Long-Term Holds

Not every stock is worth holding forever. Look for these qualities:

  • Consistent Revenue Growth: Company grows revenue year-over-year (5+ years). Check earnings reports.
  • Strong Competitive Moat: Brand power, patents, network effects, cost advantages (think Apple, Google, Amazon).
  • Healthy Balance Sheet: Low debt-to-equity ratio. More cash than debt. Can survive downturns.
  • Dividend Growth (bonus): Companies that pay AND grow dividends reward long holders (SCHD, JNJ, KO).
  • Market Leadership: #1 or #2 in their industry. Leaders tend to stay leaders.
  • You Understand the Business: If you can't explain what they do in one sentence, don't invest.

💰 Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA) — Your Best Friend

  • What: Invest the SAME dollar amount on a regular schedule (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly) regardless of price.
  • Why: Removes emotion and timing. You buy more shares when cheap, fewer when expensive. Averages out over time.
  • How on Robinhood: Set up Recurring Investments. Pick your stocks/ETFs, set amount ($25, $50, $100/week), pick your day. Done. Fully automated.
  • The Math: $100/week into VOO = $5,200/year. At 10% avg return, that's ~$86,000 in 10 years, ~$340,000 in 20 years.

🚨 When to Sell a Long-Term Hold

The goal is to hold for YEARS. But there are valid reasons to sell:

  • ✅ Sell: The company's fundamentals have CHANGED (not just stock price dipped). Revenue declining multiple quarters. Lost competitive edge.
  • ✅ Sell: You need the money for a real life expense (not to day trade with).
  • ✅ Sell: Your thesis was wrong and you can articulate WHY.
  • ❌ DON'T Sell: Stock dropped 10-20% on market-wide fear. That's a buying opportunity, not an exit.
  • ❌ DON'T Sell: You're bored or want to "trade it." Long-term means LONG TERM.
  • ❌ DON'T Sell: Some random person on Reddit/Twitter said it's overvalued.
